# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Indian government procurement officers (AE/EE level) and department decision-makers evaluating tools for the tender lifecycle. They arrive on this marketing site skeptical of AI claims, accountable to auditors, and fluent in the vocabulary of GFR 2017, CVC guidelines, and the Manual for Procurement. The job: decide in one scroll whether Scribe is credible enough to request a demo.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Scribe, an AI procurement co-pilot for India's government. Scribe drafts tenders section by section, runs a deterministic CVC pre-check, groups pre-bid queries, evaluates bids into a live comparative statement, and tracks execution — with every decision staying with the officer. The site's success metric is demo requests from government departments.

## Brand Personality

Sovereign, auditable, quietly confident. The interface practices what the product preaches: procurement artifacts shown as real documents (§ section signs, tender numbers, rule citations), statuses that behave like build pipelines (working → done), and nothing decorative that a CAG auditor would call fluff.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-SaaS landing pages: gradient blobs, glassmorphism, purple-cyan gradients, floating 3D shapes with no meaning. The user's standing instruction: "don't create something AI generic."
- AI-generated-looking imagery that doesn't match the UI palette (earlier 3D renders were desaturated on request to fit).
- Heavy black containers around media (removed from the video section on request).
- Anything that undermines legal-document credibility — the artifacts must read like real government paperwork, not mockup lorem.

## Design Principles

- **Show the product working.** The hero is the product story told as an animation (chat → draft → pre-check → bids → comparison → award). New sections should demonstrate, not claim.
- **Blueprint as texture.** The system's background language is the engineering-drawing grid (dark sections: navy + grid + pointer spotlight). Texture derives from the procurement/drawing domain, never from decoration.
- **Legal grammar is the brand voice.** § markers, rule citations (GFR 2017 · Rule 170), tender numbers, masked quotes — accuracy of register is the differentiator.
- **Status reads like CI.** Working states are filled blue with a spinner; completion flips green with a check. One grammar everywhere.
- **Approved copy only.** Site copy is reviewed via the client's feedback sheet; apply reviewed text verbatim and keep punchy titles where the sheet folds them into eyebrows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

GIGW 3.0 / UX4G and WCAG 2.1 AA (stated commitment in footer and FAQ): keyboard operation, visible focus, AA+ contrast on text, screen-reader compatibility. Every animation respects prefers-reduced-motion (entrance, spinners, auto-cycling sections all gate or degrade).
